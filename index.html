Dr. Norman Borlaug

Norman Ernest Borlaug (March 25, 1914 â€“ September 12, 2009) was an American agronomist and humanitarian who has been called "the father of the Green Revolution" for his role in initiating and developing high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties. His work saved millions from starvation in the 1960s and 1970s, and he was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970.

Borlaug's contributions to agriculture were revolutionary. His development of semi-dwarf, high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat varieties, along with improved agricultural practices, dramatically increased wheat production worldwide. This achievement was particularly impactful in countries facing food shortages.
